--- layout: default title: LTTP's Targetting System ---

The A Link to the Past speedrunning community is one of the nicest communities there is. Almost.

As with any large community, there are bad apples that can ruin the bunch. As a fun fact, that metaphor isn't inaccurate; bad apples can actually release chemicals that tell other apples they need to rot as well. And that's sort of happened here as well.

Let me just preface this with a disclaimer. I am a goddamn bully. I am not nice. I'm mean, and I hate things. Especially myself. It's not an excuse, but the reason is that I have been bullied my whole life. In the real world, I'm a frail, pathetic loser who can't fight back. My only choice is to run and hide, or accept the onslaught. On the Internet, where words have the real power, suddenly, I am on even footing. If someone says something mean, I can say something mean back. What are they going to do? They can't hit me. All they can do is say another mean thing. And I can counter just as easily. Unfortunately for me, by the time I started really using the Internet, my fists were cocked, ready to fight.

But this advantage of "we can only use words" is a double-edged sword. In the real world, people who can adequately fight back aren't bullied, because there's risk of injury involved. You need to pick on the weak to sate your blood lust. When words are the only weapon, that fighting back becomes fun; it's something the bully actually looks forward to.

Again, I am no saint. But that doesn't make me blind. And it doesn't make me immune to being a victim. As pathetic as it is to play the card, I have been a victim.

The ALTTP community doesn't have a general bullying problem. If you walk in and ask for help, as many as 10 people might chime in, trying to give you the best advice possible. You may walk away with this really good impression of everyone. If you stick around long enough, you'll see the disgusting fungus of our targetting system.

Bullying random people is not okay. You need to wait for them to establish themselves. First of all, this gives you decent ammunition. If you know how someone actually acts, it's a lot easier to make fun of them. Secondly, you need a general agreement that this person is an acceptable target. It will be all quiet on this front for a while, but then someone will fire the first shot. And then…

No response.

No one said anything. Even if they fired back, no one said, "You shouldn't have taken that shot."

Congratulations, whomever-you-are! You have just been designated the exclusive label of target. Pretty soon, people will notice that no one defends you. It will start with seemingly light-hearted jokes, but that's all it takes to eventually escalate into something you don't want. The longer you stay quiet, the worse you'll let it get. But you can't fight back either. If you even slightly toe the line, you run the risk of being labelled the aggressor yourself. You're damned if you do, and you're damned if you don't.

If "clique" weren't a tainted word in the community, I would use it. I don't want to use it, but there are definitely groups of people who hang together, and behave together. If one person decides they want to make fun of someone, their friends will join pretty easily. It's not too uncommon to see two people who have never interacted engage in a one-sided conversation of distaste and disgust. There are plenty of examples of people who have never said a single word before suddenly being jerks. While I can only readily produce evidence of it happening to myself, I don't doubt my vague memories of it happening to others.

In a sense, it's like some form of social eugenics. Granted, the community is still really open, and at least 95% of people will pass through unharmed. But if you're part of that unlucky other group, then you're weird, you're annoying, you talk funny, or maybe you just talk too much. It doesn't matter. The point is, you're unwanted. You should leave. And there's a friendly group of people who are willing to let you know that.

I wrote this in response to recent attacks against me, but I decided to wait until I was in a more rational state of mind, as I didn't want this to be reactionary bawling. I do not regret writing this, and I see it as a valid observation on the community describing an issue not pertaining only to myself.
